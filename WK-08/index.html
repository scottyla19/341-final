NBA Playoffs Recap: Tuesday April 18, 2017

Chicago Bulls: 111 @ Boston Celtics: 97

Team 1Q 2Q 3Q 4Q Total
Chicago 31 23 32 25 111
Boston 26 20 29 22 97

The Bulls were led by playoff veteran Dwayne Wade and his 22 points. While veteran Rajon Rondo held Boston's prolific scorer, Isiah Thomas to 20 points and also almost tallied a triple doule himself (11 points-9 rebounds-14 assists). Jimmy Butler added 22 points for the Bulls. The Celtics are now down 0-2 in the best of seven series after losing the first two games at home. The Celtics need to find some of the defensive prowess and intensity that they possessed during the regular season if they want to find a way to move on in the playoffs.

Milwaukee Bucks: 100 @ Toronto Raptors: 106

Team 1Q 2Q 3Q 4Q Total
Milwaukee 25 27 31 17 100
Toronto 28 27 29 22 106

Kyle Lowry bounced back from his Game 1 struggles to lead Toronto with 22 points and a series tying win at home. Demar DeRozan added 23 for the Raptors while Giannis "The Greek Freak" Antetokounmpo tallied 24 points and 15 rebounds in the loss for the Bucks. The Bucks had a chance to win another game in Toronto but couln't get their shots to fall down the stretch. The series is tied 1-1 as the teams head to Milwaukee for two games.

Utah Jazz: 91 @ Los Angeles Clippers: 99

Team 1Q 2Q 3Q 4Q Total
Utah 18 24 28 21 91
Los Angeles 29 22 28 20 99

The Clippers manhandled the Jazz from the opening tip to the final buzzer. Blake Griffin led the Clippers wit 24 points while DeAndre Jordan pitched in 18 points and 15 rebounds and Chris Paul added 21 points and 10 assists. The Jazz never led during the game and their only bright spot was Gordon Heyward's 20 points. It's not all grim for the Jazz, as they at least salvaged a split on the Clippers homecourt. The series is tied 1-1 going back to Utah for the next two games.
